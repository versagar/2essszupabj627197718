What is Mechanical Robotics?

For this age group, our focus is on the 'mechanical' part. This isn't about computer coding or complex programming. Instead, it's about helping children understand how things move and work in the physical world.

Key Concepts:

  • Gears: How they turn and make things move.
  • Levers: How they can lift heavy objects.
  • Wheels and Axles: How they create motion.
  • Pulleys: How they make it easier to pull things up.

Our Play-Based Approach

Our curriculum is designed around one simple principle: children learn best when they are playing. Each class is a fun challenge or an engaging story, not a lecture. The entire process is about discovery and having fun, not about memorizing facts.

Lesson Plan: Levers & Pulleys

  • The Makerzoid See-Saw: Learn about levers and balance by building a working seesaw.
  • The Mighty Crane: Use a pulley and a lever to build a crane and lift small objects.
  • The Makerzoid Catapult: Discover stored energy and the power of a lever in a fun, dynamic way.
  • The Wrecking Ball: Build a structure with a wrecking ball attached to a pulley system.

Lesson Plan: Wheels & Axles

  • The Makerzoid Race Car: Design a car and learn about wheels and axles for smooth rolling.
  • The Shopping Cart: Understand how wheels and axles support weight and allow easy movement.
  • The Makerzoid Tractor: Explore how different wheel sizes affect movement and speed.
  • The Rolling Monster: Learn that more wheels can provide stability.

Lesson Plan: Gears

  • The Spinning Robot: Use a series of gears to make a robot's arms spin.
  • The Mixer: See how gears can change the direction of motion.
  • The Gearbox: Learn that you can change the speed of rotation with different-sized gears.
  • The Clock: Visualize how gears work together in a sequence to make the hands move.
  • The Ferris Wheel: Observe how a large gear can power a slow, steady rotation.

Lesson Plan: Structures & Building

  • The Makerzoid Tower: Learn about structural stability and the importance of a wide base.
  • The Strong Bridge: Test how a bridge can support weight and learn about support structures.
  • The Archway: Discover how curved structures can distribute weight and be very strong.
  • The Makerzoid House: Understand how walls, roofs, and foundations work together.

Lesson Plan: Combining Concepts

  • The Hungry Alligator: Combines a lever to open the mouth and gears to make the motion smoother.
  • The Motorized Car: Uses gears to transfer power from an axle to the wheels.
  • The Robotic Hand: Uses a series of levers and strings to create a hand that can open and close.
  • The Makerzoid Windmill: Uses gears to transfer the turning motion of the blades.
  • The Playground: A larger project where they can build a playground with multiple simple machines.
