Course and its objectives:


"Mechanical Robotics is an exciting hands-on course designed for students of all ages to explore the fascinating world of mechanical systems, machines, and robotics. Using a mix of simple machines, gears, and robotic kits, we’ll dive into practical concepts that teach engineering, problem-solving, and creativity. Whether you're 4 or 14, this course will introduce you to the power of mechanics in the world of robotics."


Course Concepts of Mechanics

Age groups:

  • Ages 4-6: Introduction to basic machines and movement
  • Ages 6-9: Hands-on exploration of mechanical systems
  • Ages 9-12: Building and optimizing functional machines
  • Ages 12+: Advanced mechanical concepts and robotics



Learning Outcomes (What will students learn?):

At TWC Classrooms, we recognize that every student is unique, with individual learning styles, abilities, and interests. That’s why we offer personalized learning journeys that cater to all age groups and skill levels.
Our courses are designed to provide each learner with a well-rounded understanding of mechanical engineering and robotics, ensuring they achieve the following outcomes:

For All Age Groups ( Starting from 4 year old):

By the end of the course, students will have:

  • Developed a Strong Foundation in Mechanics & Robotics: Students will gain essential knowledge about mechanical systems, robotics, and how they interact with the world around them.
  • Hands-On Experience: Through building and experimenting with a variety of models and robots, students will gain practical, real-world experience in designing, creating, and testing their own machines.
  • Enhanced Problem-Solving Abilities: Students will learn how to approach challenges logically and creatively, developing their problem-solving skills as they troubleshoot and refine their designs.
  • Boosted Critical Thinking & Analytical Skills: With each project and activity, students will refine their ability to think critically, analyze situations, and apply their knowledge in innovative ways.
  • Improved Collaboration & Communication: Working in teams or individually, students will develop strong collaboration and communication skills, sharing ideas, explaining concepts, and learning from each other.
  • Prepared for Future STEM Endeavors: By mastering the core principles of engineering, mechanics, and robotics, students will be well-prepared for future studies in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) fields.
For Different Age Groups (Tailored Learning):
4-6 Year Olds:
Students will begin their journey with hands-on exploration, fostering curiosity and laying the groundwork for understanding basic mechanical concepts through play and simple activities.
6-9 Year Olds:
As students grow, they will refine their understanding of how machines work, developing more structured problem-solving skills and introducing basic engineering design principles through creative activities.
9-12 Year Olds:
Students will begin to tackle complex mechanical systems and understand efficiency, gaining a deeper appreciation for the role of mechanics in everyday life and starting to apply these concepts to more advanced projects.
12+ Year Olds:
Older students will gain exposure to advanced mechanical systems, including robotics and automation, and will learn to combine mechanical design with programming, equipping them for further studies in engineering or robotics.
    Skills Gained:
    Across all age groups, students will develop the following core skills:
  • Mechanical Design & Engineering: The ability to conceptualize, design, and create functional mechanical systems.
  • Critical Thinking & Problem Solving: Developing solutions through logical reasoning, creativity, and experimentation.
  • Creativity & Innovation: Using imagination and inventiveness to build and improve machines and robots.
  • Technical Communication: Effectively presenting and explaining their ideas and projects, both in written and verbal form.
  • Collaboration & Teamwork: Learning to work both independently and as part of a team, fostering strong communication and cooperative problem-solving skills.
  • Hands-On Learning: Gaining practical skills in building, testing, and iterating on mechanical systems, both manually and with programmable components.

Overall Development:

By the end of the course, students will not only understand how things work but will also have developed the skills and confidence to bring their own mechanical creations to life. Our hands-on, interactive approach ensures that each student, regardless of age or experience level, leaves with a strong foundation in mechanics, ready for future STEM challenges and opportunities.

FAQ Section:

Q1: What tools or kits are used in the course?
We use a variety of tools and kits based on the age group. For example, younger students use Makerzoid Smart Robots, Lego Simple & Powered Machines, etc while older students work with LEGO SPIKE Prime, WeDo 2.0, LEGO EV3, etc.
Q2: Do I need to buy any materials?
You need to buy the kits related to the course. You can buy any kit similar to the recommended ones, and from any place.
Q3: Can I take this course if I have no prior experience with robotics?
Absolutely! The course is designed for beginners as well and provides step-by-step guidance for every age level. We have personalized the learning so that anyone can get benefits from the courses.
Q4: What if I miss a class?
There will be a chance to reschedule in an ongoing batch.
